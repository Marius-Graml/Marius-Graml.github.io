🔬 Bachelor's Thesis: Optical Sensing through Metasurface Dispersion Spectroscopy

Completed

Developed a high-sensitivity sensor capable of detecting resonance shifts below 1 nm to measure extremely low analyte concentrations. The sensor leveraged metasurface dispersion spectroscopy as a novel optical sensing method. The project involved extensive noise analysis, optimization of the signal-to-noise ratio through a custom serial AOM configuration, and real-signal validation with hydrogen cyanide measurements. Results demonstrated competitive or superior performance compared to state-of-the-art dispersion spectroscopy systems.
